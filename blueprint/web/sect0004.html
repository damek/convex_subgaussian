3.2 The sharp stop-loss envelope

Theorem 6 Sharp stop-loss envelope under the sub-Gaussian cap
✓
#

Lean declarations

  • stopLoss_envelope

Assume that \(\mu \) is centered and satisfies the tail cap \(\mathbb {P}_\mu (|x|{\gt}t)\le s_G(t)\) for every \(t \ge 0\). Then for every \(u \ge 0\),

\[ \mathrm{stopLoss}_\mu (u) \le J_G(u), \]

where \(J_G\) is the explicit envelope built from the constants \(B\), \(a\), and \(p_0\).

Proof ▶

The proof splits into the two regions \(u \le a\) and \(u \ge a\). For \(u \le a\), the mean-zero condition and the tail cap bound the positive part of the first moment by \(B\), which gives the affine upper bound \(B - p_0 u\). For \(u \ge a\), the layer-cake identity rewrites the stop-loss as a tail integral and the cap is integrated directly. The Lean code records the two cases separately and then combines them into the single envelope formula.

Theorem 7 Extension from nonnegative thresholds to all thresholds
✓
#

Lean declarations

  • stopLoss_extend_all_u

The stop-loss control extends from \(u \ge 0\) to every real threshold \(u\) by applying the same envelope argument to \(-X\) and then using the mean-zero identity relating the stop-loss transforms of \(X\) and \(-X\).

Proof ▶

The proof is one-dimensional and elementary: once the bound is known for nonnegative thresholds, the negative-threshold case is rewritten in terms of the positive-threshold stop-loss of \(-X\). Because the tail assumption is symmetric and the mean is zero, the transformed problem satisfies the same hypotheses.
