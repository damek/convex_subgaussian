2.2 Comparison and sharpness statements

The endpoint theorem is assembled from one comparison statement and one sharpness statement.

Theorem 3 Named comparison and sharpness results
✓
#

Lean declarations

  • gaussianSharpness_below_c0

The theorem gaussianDomination_c0 is the comparison statement at the optimal Gaussian scale: it shows that the Gaussian law at scale \(c_0\) dominates every admissible random variable against every convex integrable test function. The theorem gaussianSharpness_below_c0 proves that every smaller positive scale \(c {\lt} c_0\) fails already for the canonical witness measure \(\mu ^\star \) and a hinge test function of the form \(x \mapsto (x-u)_+\).

Proof ▶

The proof infrastructure for these two statements lives in the internal analytic module. The public wrapper gaussianDomination_c0 pushes a random variable \(X\) forward to its law, checks the mean-zero and tail hypotheses on that law, and then invokes the internal measure-theoretic domination theorem. The public sharpness theorem packages the explicit extremal witness already constructed in Lean and rewrites its stop-loss strictness into the reader-facing hinge-integral inequality.
