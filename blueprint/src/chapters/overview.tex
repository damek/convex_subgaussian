\chapter{Overview}\label{chap:overview}

This blueprint describes the proof of the sharp one-dimensional
Gaussian convex comparison theorem: the smallest constant $c_0$ such that
every centered real random variable with two-sided sub-Gaussian tails
$\Prb(|X|>t)\le 2e^{-t^2/2}$ is dominated in convex order by $c_0 G$,
where $G\sim\mathcal N(0,1)$.
The constant $c_0$ is determined by an explicit system of equations
and is attained by a canonical extremal distribution that saturates the
tail constraint.

Every theorem cited here is formalized in Lean~4 without \texttt{sorry}.
